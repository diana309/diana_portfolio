About Vita

Vita is a data-driven responsive web platform that helps users learn about vitamins and minerals that come from natural sources. Vita has a built-in nutrition calculator to get customized suggestions of fruits and vegetables that can help the users' diet.

Project information

Duration:

12 weeks

My role:

Project Manager & UX Designer

Team size:

3 Developers & 4 Designers

Platform:

Data-driven Web Application

The Problem

Vitamins and minerals are vital for our health but understanding which vitamins and minerals are the most beneficial can sometimes be unclear. It is also difficult to know which natural food items have the nutrients humans specifically need in their diet, and, relying too much on supplements can not only be expensive, but can lead to problems related to vitamin overdose.

The Solution

Vita is a platform that helps users learn about vitamins and minerals that come from natural sources. Vita has a built-in nutrition calculator to get customized suggestions of fruits and vegetables that can help the usersâ€™ diet. Unlike other typical nutrient apps showing calorie needs and meal plans, Vita focuses on how the users can use fruits and vegetables to help improve their health.

My Role

In this project, I was the project manager and a UX designer. I worked on creating the home page and nutrient calculator feature. I created a custom API to show results for 20 fruits, 20 vegetables, 10 vitamins and 11 minerals. I was also responsible for creating the final version of the user flow, 5 high fidelity screens and the marketing materials.

User Flow

Wireframes

Home Page

Home page has three main sections: search by fruit or vegetable name, search by vitamin or mineral name and subscribe to the newsletters. For the fruit and vegetable search our team scraped data from an open source website and created our custom API where we added our own illustrations, and short descriptions. Each vitamin and mineral is displayed on a doughnut chart to signify their importance as part of a balanced diet.

Single Fruit Info

The vitamins and minerals contained in each fruit and vegetable will be displayed on a single page. There is also an option to click on other fruits that have similar nutritional characteristics. The main feature of the application is the Nutrient Calculator, that allows users to input some information about their diet to get recommendations of which fruit or vegetable they should consume more.

Nutrient Calculator Results

After the user inputs their diet information they will get two charts that will tell them how much of each vitamin and mineral they will need to consume on a daily basis to keep a healthy diet. They can also click on a chart or on the table next to the chart to get 4 suggestions of what foods has a lot of this particular mineral or vitamins (eg. see iron rich food). The user can also select their health concerns in the section below to get even more customized recommendations. Some of the users might not want to share their health concerns this is why we kept this section optional.

The challenge was figuring out how we could display the vitamin and mineral results, as the range of numbers was very wide and would not look well on a chart. We have decided to remove vitamins and minerals that had a very small number from the chart but keep it in the table.

System Architecture

Our frontend utilizes modern technologies like ReactJS, Sass and Nivo charts. Backend is built up of NodeJs to connect with MongoDB and endpoints to various datasets are exposed using ExpressJS. The whole package of front and backend is hosted on AWS. Web Scraper (written in python) is used to connect to third party websites and provide dataset which is passed to node and then further transferred to the react module to display on the front end.

Project Managment

For project management we used Asana. After each team meeting I was responsible for assigning tasks that we have agreed to, for each team member. For each task I wrote a short explanation and provided a checklist to make sure nothing is missing. I have also color coded different teams and added tags so that it was easier to filter the tasks. We also agreed to write any questions in the comments under the tasks so that every member could answer.

Retrospective

It has been a pleasant experience working on the project. Our team has improved a lot in terms of clear communication, having more efficient meetings and giving constructive feedback. At first we struggled a lot when it came to giving strong feedback as we were afraid of making someone upset, however later in the project we learned how to do it in a professional manner.

What can be improved

We neglected user testing in this project, as we were running out of time. As a result, we only tested with 2 users. When users started to use the platform after we launched the project, we received a lot of feedback regarding certain parts that were unclear to them. The lesson learned is that it's very important to create a functional prototype and test it before starting development.
